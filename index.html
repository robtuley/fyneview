Fyne View

Where is it?

Fyne View, Shore Road, Strachur, PA27 8BX

As you drive into Strachur from the A83, the turning onto Shore Road is on your right at a sharp left-hand bend (A886 signposted to Portavadie). The house is just after the end of the short single-track section, if you reach the war memorial you've gone too far.

In the House

When you arrive

  • Keys for the back door, garage etc are hanging in the cupboard next to the back door;
  • Turn water on - the key is in the garage, hanging up on the left by the side door. The water stopcock is just outside the back door. Push the metal cover to the side and turn water on with ¼-turn in either direction Turn off all the taps upstairs and downstairs once the water is turned on;
  • The upstairs loo may need flushing once or twice to start with if the cistern keeps running;
  • The open fire is set and there's a lighter on the mantelpiece. Please take the polythene 'plug' out of the chimney carefully first! There is a plastic bag in the garage for the ash. There is a small shovel, broom head and old towel in the garage for cleaning the fire out with. There's also usually plenty of paper, wood and an axe in the garage for fire lighting.

Top Tips

  • The shower and hot water switches are in the cupboard by the back door;
  • There's a fan heater in the bathroom: 1 pull = 1kW, 2 pulls = 2kW, 3 pulls = off;
  • The electric panel heaters are all set to frost protection. Adjust to suit. The heaters in the bedrooms have 2 dials. The one on the left should remain set to the 'full sun' setting at all times;
  • The free standing heater in the living room has a timer that can be set;
  • Spare bedding and towels are in drawers under the bed in the front bedroom. Use as necessary, but please wash anything left in the washing basket from the last visit and put away anything left out to dry. Leave dirty bedding etc in the basket in the front bedroom.

When you leave, you must

  • Turn off the water (¼-turn in either direction);
  • Open the taps
  • Leave the panel heaters on, set to frost protection.

(these are required as conditions of the insurance)

Also...

  • Please clean up - the vacuum cleaner is in the cupboard under the oven and other cleaning stuff under the sink;
  • Leave the fire as you found it for the next people visiting;
  • Turn the hot water off as soon as everyone has showered on the last day so you don't leave too much hot water in the tank;
  • Turn off the free standing heater in the living room (don't get caught out by the timer!);
  • Turn off the power to the showers, but leave the mains power on;
  • Take your rubbish away with you.

Food and Drink

The Creggans Inn on the road into Strachur serves a decent pint, and good bar/restaurant meals. Critically, it is within easy walking distance of the house making it a firm favourite.

The Cairndow Oyster Bar on the A83 towards Inveraray is the original Loch Fyne fish restaurant. It makes a good lunch spot; but closes early so not great for dinner.

In Inveraray itself, the George Hotel is a lively location for quality pub grub. There's often live music, and it can get very busy in the summer.

The Whistlefield Inn just off the A815 towards Dunoon is a cosy tavern for monster portions of pub grub.

The Oyster Catcher at Otter Ferry is a little further off the beaten track in a beautiful location on the loch-shore, and serves good food.

Sunny Day

Walking

The "Arrochar Alps" are nearby, with stunning views on a clear day: most walks start from Arrochar or Inveruglas. The Tyndrum Hills and Crianlarich Hills are also close.

For a short local walk, clearly way-marked routes start from Glenbranter, a quick drive towards Dunoon.

Further along Loch Eck are the (large!) Botanic Gardens and Puck's Glen, both excellent sunday afternoon walks.

There are a number of books and maps in the house to help out with other ideas.

Cycling

Glenbranter has a short downhill single track mountain bike trail which you can cycle to straight from the house.

The Ardgarten mountain bike trails are a short drive towards Arrochar.

Lots of quiet roads mean plenty of options on a road bike too.

Exploring

The Isle of Bute is merely a ferry-ride away from Colintraive.

The Cowal Peninsula has a number of interesting villages and impressive views so is well worth an explore.

There are a few different wildlife-viewing/island-viewing (or both!) boat trips from Oban.
